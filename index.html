My mother, Sunita Devi, opened Mithila Threads in 1988 with ₹8,000 borrowed from her mother-in-law. She sourced muslin from a weaver collective in Santipur and silk from a supplier in Malda who still works with her granddaughter. 37 years later, we're still in the same 12-foot shop in Bowbazar, still sourcing from the same families. Our Story

Three Generations,
One Weaving Community

My grandmother, Kamla Devi, was a weaver in Santipur. She made dhotis and gamchas for the local market. In the 1970s she started experimenting with tussar silk and got a reputation for doing saree pallus that other weavers couldn't replicate. My mother, Sunita Devi, saw that the market in Kolkata had people who wanted handloom but didn't have access to it directly. She opened Mithila Threads in Bowbazar in 1988 to sell her mother's work — and work from three other weaver families she knew personally.

Today we represent 11 weaver families across Bengal and Bihar. We don't do 'custom designs' that get sent to powerloom factories. Everything we sell is genuinely handwoven. I know every weaver family by name, their villages, and the specific techniques they use. When you buy from Mithila Threads, you are buying directly from a specific handloom community, not a brand that sources from one.

"I could expand if I wanted to. But if I expand, I can't guarantee every piece is handwoven by the families I know. I'd rather stay small and honest than big and compromised."
